### What I’d add or refine

1. **Platform dynamics** — Platform risk (API changes, pricing, competition) is a major concern for SaaS builders but isn’t in your summary.
2. **Cost and capital efficiency** — Burn discipline, lean teams, and “default alive” models are heavily emphasized in 2025–2026.
3. **AI cost structure** — Heavy use of LLMs affects unit economics, margins, and pricing; this supports the usage-based pricing trend but is worth calling out explicitly.
4. **Distributed / remote teams** — Many modern SaaS teams are remote-first and tooling-heavy; this is often linked to micro-SaaS and niche products.

### On the “75% per Gartner” claim

Your skepticism about the “up to 75%” low-code/no-code claim is reasonable. Such numbers are often from narrow benchmarks, vendor reports, or broad definitions of “faster.” Without the original source, treating it as directionally true rather than precise is appropriate.

For single-source research, your structure (trends → recommendations → limitations) is strong. You avoid overclaiming, and your recommendations follow logically from the trends. The main weakness is the lack of cross-model comparison and the gap round capture failure, which you already highlight. The substance of the trends is plausible; additional sources would mainly add confirmation, nuance, and weight rather than fundamentally change the picture.
